## Personal and Academic Profile

Dr. Oded Y. Steinberg is a modern historian and Senior Lecturer at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, affiliated with the Department of International Relations and the European Forum. His research focuses on intellectual history, Anglo-German thought, periodization, race and nationalism, and humanitarian advocacy in the nineteenth century.

## Higher Education

- **2003-2006:** Undergraduate studies at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem (History, Jewish History, BA program for excelling students), magna cum laude.
- **2007-2010:** MA studies at the European Forum, Hebrew University of Jerusalem. Supervisor: Prof. Dan Diner. Magna cum laude.
- **2011-2015:** DPhil in History, Lincoln College, University of Oxford. Supervisor: Prof. Oliver Zimmer.

## Postdoctoral Training

- **2015-2017:** Joint postdoctoral fellow, Freie Universitat Berlin (Friedrich Meinecke Institute of History) and the Hebrew University of Jerusalem.
- **2017-2019:** Postdoctoral fellow, Hubert H. Humphrey Center for Social Research and Department of History, Ben-Gurion University of the Negev.
- **2019-2020:** Postdoctoral fellow, CSoC - Center for the Study of Conversion and Inter-Religious Encounters.

## Teaching and Supervision

Dr. Steinberg supervises MA and doctoral research in modern European and international history, nationalism, and related intellectual traditions.

Recent supervision includes MA theses on German nationalism, comparative political thought, and European intellectual history, as well as co-supervision of doctoral students.
